Career & Biography

Dr Mesfin Genie is a Continuing Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Health Economics at Newcastle Business School, University of Newcastle, Australia, and a Measurement & Regulatory Science Fellow at Duke University. He earned his PhD in Economics from Ca’ Foscari University of Venice (2019), using stated-preference techniques to study healthcare decisions. He holds an MSc in Health Economics & Management (University of Bologna, 2015), an MSc in Economics (Addis Ababa University, 2010), and a BA in Economics (Jimma University, 2006). He has held positions at Ca’ Foscari, University of Aberdeen, Auburn University, and Duke University.

Research Focus

  • Patient Preferences & Behavioural Economics: Discrete Choice Experiments in contexts from organ transplantation to vaccines.
  • Information Processing: Eye-tracking plus DCEs to reveal attention to cost and attributes.
  • Outcome Measurement: Patient-centred metrics and decision aids aligning policy with values.
