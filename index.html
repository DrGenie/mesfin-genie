Career & Biography

Dr Mesfin Genie is a Continuing Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Health Economics at Newcastle Business School, University of Newcastle, Australia, and a Measurement & Regulatory Science Fellow at Duke University. He earned his PhD in Economics from Ca’ Foscari University of Venice (2019), using stated-preference techniques to study healthcare decisions. He holds an MSc in Health Economics & Management (University of Bologna, 2015), an MSc in Economics (Addis Ababa University, 2010), and a BA in Economics (Jimma University, 2006). He has held positions at Ca’ Foscari, University of Aberdeen, Auburn University, and Duke University.

Research Interests

  • Discrete Choice Experiments (DCEs): Designing and analysing choice data to quantify patient preferences.
  • Eye-Tracking: Integrating eye-tracking metrics with DCEs to understand attention and information processing.
  • Behavioural Economics: Applying psychological insights to health decision-making.
  • Vaccine Mandates & Policy Evaluation: Assessing the impact and trade-offs of public-health interventions.
  • Equity–Efficiency Trade-Offs: Incorporating fairness considerations into value-based assessments.

Teaching

  • ECON3111 Behavioural Economics (2025): Introduction to behavioural economic theories and applications in health; includes in-class experiments and real-world policy tasks.
  • ECON2112 Health Economics & Finance (2025): Covers economic evaluation methods, healthcare financing models, and cost–benefit analysis in health policy.
  • ECON3011 Introduction to Health Economics & Finance (2024): Foundations of health economic theory; students apply discrete choice techniques to case studies (CES 4.5/5).
