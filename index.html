Career & Biography

Dr Mesfin Genie is a distinguished health economist and Continuing Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at the Newcastle Business School, University of Newcastle, Australia. He also serves as a Measurement & Regulatory Science Fellow at Duke University, USA, where he contributes to cutting-edge research in health policy and decision-making. With a PhD in Economics from Ca’ Foscari University of Venice (2019), Dr Genie specializes in stated-preference techniques, particularly discrete choice experiments (DCEs), to explore healthcare decision-making processes. His academic journey includes an MSc in Health Economics & Management from the University of Bologna (2015), an MSc in Economics from Addis Ababa University (2010), and a BA in Economics from Jimma University (2006).

Prior to his current role, Dr Genie held research and teaching positions at prestigious institutions, including Ca’ Foscari University of Venice, the University of Aberdeen, Auburn University, and Duke University. His work bridges academic research and policy application, focusing on improving health outcomes through evidence-based interventions. Dr Genie’s contributions have been recognized internationally, with his research informing public health policies in multiple countries.

Research Interests

Dr Genie’s research lies at the intersection of health economics, behavioral science, and public policy, with a focus on understanding and optimizing decision-making in healthcare. His work leverages advanced methodologies to address pressing global health challenges. Key areas of expertise include:

  • Discrete Choice Experiments (DCEs): Designing and analyzing choice experiments to quantify patient and public preferences for healthcare interventions, enabling personalized and effective policy design.
  • Eye-Tracking in Decision-Making: Integrating eye-tracking technology with DCEs to study attention and information processing, providing insights into how individuals prioritize health-related information.
  • Behavioral Economics: Applying psychological insights to understand and influence health decision-making, particularly in the context of vaccine uptake and public health compliance.
  • Vaccine Mandates & Policy Evaluation: Assessing the impact, trade-offs, and public acceptability of vaccine policies and other public health interventions, with a focus on pandemics like COVID-19.
  • Equity–Efficiency Trade-Offs: Developing frameworks to incorporate fairness and equity considerations into value-based healthcare assessments, ensuring inclusive policy outcomes.

Teaching

Dr Genie is a dedicated educator, committed to fostering critical thinking and analytical skills in his students. His teaching portfolio at the University of Newcastle includes courses that blend economic theory with practical applications in health policy and behavioral science. His courses are designed to equip students with the tools to address real-world health challenges.

  • ECON3111 Behavioural Economics (2025): This course introduces students to behavioral economic theories and their applications in health contexts. Through hands-on experiments and policy simulations, students explore how psychological factors influence decision-making, with a focus on designing effective health interventions.
  • ECON2112 Health Economics & Finance (2025): A comprehensive course covering economic evaluation techniques, healthcare financing models, and cost-benefit analysis. Students learn to assess the economic viability of health policies and interventions, preparing them for careers in health policy analysis.
  • ECON3011 Introduction to Health Economics & Finance (2024): This foundational course explores health economic theory, with students conducting discrete choice case studies to understand patient preferences. Rated highly by students (CES 4.5/5), it emphasizes practical applications in health policy.

Key Projects

Dr Genie leads and contributes to transformative research projects that address critical health policy challenges. His projects combine rigorous economic analysis with practical policy implications.

  • MandEval: Evaluation of COVID-19 Vaccine Mandates (MRFF): A comprehensive study assessing the effectiveness and public acceptability of vaccine mandates during the COVID-19 pandemic, informing future policy design.
  • FETP India: Field Epidemiology Training Program Decision Aid (World Bank): Developing decision-making tools to enhance epidemiological training in India, strengthening public health capacity.
  • Loneliness Support Interventions Among Older Adults: Investigating preferences for support programs to reduce loneliness, with a focus on equitable access for aging populations.
  • Telehealth Policy Evolution Post-Pandemic: Analyzing the adoption and impact of telehealth in Australia, providing insights for sustainable healthcare delivery models.
